1999 in literature

==Events==
*May 1 – Andrew Motion is appointed Poet Laureate of the United Kingdom for ten years.<ref></ref>
*June 19 – Stephen King is hit by a van while taking a walk. He is hospitalized for three weeks and only resumes writing his next book, On Writing, in July.<ref></ref>
*September 7 – Black Diamond, designed by Schmidt Hammer Lassen Architects, is inaugurated as an extension to the Royal Danish Library in Copenhagen.<ref></ref>
*unknown date – Persephone Books is founded in Bloomsbury, London, by Nicola Beauman, to reprint mid-20th century fiction and non-fiction, mainly by women.<ref></ref>
