== Africa ==
===South Africa===
thumb
Note that most of the heritage railway operators in South Africa have their own depots where locomotives and coaches are kept and serviced, but run on state-owned railways.
* Atlantic Rail – Now defunct. Formally ran day trips from Cape Town to Simonstown using steam locomotives and heritage coaching stock<ref> urltoken Atlantic Rail</ref>
* Friends of the Rail – day trips from Hermanstad (Pretoria) using steam locomotives and heritage coaching stock<ref> urltoken Friends of the Rail</ref>
* Outeniqua Choo Tjoe – A heritage railway that has not operated since August 2006.<ref></ref>
* Patons Country Narrow Gauge Railway – a two-foot narrow gauge heritage railway in KwaZulu-Natal, South Africa, from Ixopo to Umzimkhulu
* Reefsteamers – day trips from Johannesburg to Magaliesburg.<ref> urltoken Reefsteamers</ref>
* Rovos Rail – up-market railtours<ref> urltoken Rovos Rail</ref>
* The Sandstone Heritage Trust – private railway operating 2 foot gauge steam locomotives<ref> urltoken The Sandstone Heritage Trust</ref>
* Umgeni Steam Railway – Kloof to Inchanga, near Durban<ref> urltoken Steam Railway </ref>

This list of heritage railways is a listing of heritage railways sorted by country, state or region. A heritage railway is a preserved or tourist railroad which is run as a tourist attraction, is usually but not always run by volunteers, and often seeks to re-create railway scenes of the past.
