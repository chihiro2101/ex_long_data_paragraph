2000 in literature

==Events==
*February – El Ateneo Grand Splendid bookstore takes over the Teatro Gran Splendid in Buenos Aires, converting it for use as retail space.<ref></ref>
*February 13 – The final original Peanuts comic strip is published.
*March 14 – Stephen King's novella Riding the Bullet is published in e-book format only, as the world's first mass-market electronic book.<ref></ref>
*September 26 – English politician and writer Jeffrey Archer is charged with perjury, and on the same day opens in the title role of his own courtroom drama, The Accused.<ref></ref>
*December 15 – In a landmark censorship case, Little Sisters Book and Art Emporium v. Canada (Minister of Justice), the Supreme Court of Canada rules that Canada Customs has no authority to make judgments on the permissibility of material being shipped to retailers, only to confiscate material specifically ruled by the courts to constitute an offence under the Canadian Criminal Code.
