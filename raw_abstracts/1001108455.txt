Little Darlings is a 1980 American teen comedy-drama starring Tatum O'Neal and Kristy McNichol and featuring Armand Assante and Matt Dillon. It was directed by Ronald F. Maxwell.<ref> urltoken The New York Times, Little Darlings (1980) Overview</ref> The screenplay was written by Kimi Peck and Dalene Young and the original music score was composed by Charles Fox. The film was marketed with the tagline "Don't let the title fool you", a reference to a scene in which Randy comments on Angel's name, to which Angel replies, "Don't let the name fool you."
Critic Roger Ebert said of the film that it "somehow does succeed in treating the awesome and scary subject of sexual initiation with some of the dignity it deserves."<ref> urltoken Little Darlings By Roger Ebert, rogerebert.com, March 25, 1980</ref>
