The title of Duke of Kent has been created several times in the peerages of Great Britain and the United Kingdom, most recently as a royal dukedom for the fourth son of King George&nbsp;V. Since 1942, the title has been held by Prince Edward (born 1935), Queen Elizabeth II's cousin.
