The balafon is a gourd-resonated xylophone, a type of struck idiophone.<ref name=":0"></ref> It is closely associated with the neighbouring Mandé, Senoufo and Gur peoples of West Africa,<ref name=":0" /><ref name=":1"></ref> particularly the Guinean branch of the Mandinka ethnic group,<ref></ref> but is now found across West Africa from Guinea to Mali.<ref name=":1" /> Its common name, balafon, is likely a European coinage combining its Mandinka name bala with the word fôn 'to speak'<ref name=":1" /><ref name=":2"></ref> or the Greek root phono.<ref name=":0" />
