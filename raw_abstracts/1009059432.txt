Temazepam, sold under the brand names Restoril among others, is a medication used to treat insomnia.<ref name=AHFS2019/> Such use should generally be for less than ten days.<ref name=AHFS2019/> It is taken by mouth.<ref name=AHFS2019></ref> Effects generally begin within an hour and last for up to eight hours.<ref name=Shell2015></ref>
Common side effects include sleepiness, anxiety, confusion, and dizziness.<ref name=AHFS2019/> Serious side effects may include hallucinations, abuse, anaphylaxis, and suicide.<ref name=AHFS2019/> Use is generally not recommended together with opioids.<ref name=AHFS2019/> If the dose is rapidly decreased withdrawal may occur.<ref name=AHFS2019/> Use during pregnancy or breastfeeding is not recommended.<ref name=Preg2019></ref> Temazepam is an intermediate acting benzodiazepine and hypnotic.<ref name=AHFS2019/><ref name=Shell2015/> It works by affecting GABA within the brain.<ref name=AHFS2019/>
Temazepam was patented in 1962 and came into medical use in 1969.<ref name=Fis2006></ref> It is available as a generic medication.<ref name=BNF76></ref> In 2017, it was the 142nd most commonly prescribed medication in the United States, with more than four million prescriptions.<ref></ref><ref></ref>
