Taphonomy is the study of how organisms decay and become fossilized or preserved in the archaeological record. The term taphonomy (from the Greek taphos, τάφος meaning "burial", and nomos, νόμος meaning "law") was introduced to paleontology in 1940<ref name="Lyman 1–162"></ref> by Soviet scientist Ivan Efremov to describe the study of the transition of remains, parts, or products of organisms from the biosphere to the lithosphere.<ref name="Efremov85"></ref><ref>Martin, Ronald E. urltoken (1999) "1.1 The foundations of taphonomy" Taphonomy: A Process Approach Cambridge University Press, Cambridge, England, p. 1, </ref>
