Radburn is an unincorporated community located within Fair Lawn in Bergen County, New Jersey, United States.<ref> urltoken Locality Search, State of New Jersey. Accessed March 7, 2015.</ref>
Radburn was founded in 1929 as "a town for the motor age".<ref name=history> urltoken History from the Radburn Association website</ref> Its planners, Clarence Stein and Henry Wright, and its landscape architect Marjorie Sewell Cautley<ref> urltoken Marjorie L. Sewell Cautley, Landscape Architect to the Garden City Movement by Thaisa Way, accessed June 7, 2006</ref> aimed to incorporate modern planning principles, which were then being introduced into England's Garden Cities, following ideas advocated by urban planners Ebenezer Howard, Sir Patrick Geddes<ref name=intro> urltoken Introduction from the Radburn Association website</ref> and Clarence Perry. Perry's neighbourhood unit concept was well-formulated by the time Radburn was planned, being informed by Forest Hills Gardens, Queens, New York (1909â€“1914), a garden-city development of the Russell Sage Foundation.
Radburn was explicitly designed to separate traffic by mode,<ref name=intro/> with a pedestrian path system that does not cross any major roads at grade. Radburn introduced the largely residential "superblock" and is credited with incorporating some of the earliest cul-de-sac in the United States.<ref> urltoken Cul-de-Sacs: Suburban Dream or Dead End?, a June 2006 National Public Radio story</ref>
