Midi-Pyrénées ; or ; is a former regions of France of France. Since 1 January 2016, it has been part of the new region Occitanie.<ref name="reform law"></ref> It was the largest region of Metropolitan France by area, larger than the Netherlands or Denmark.
Midi-Pyrénées has no historical or geographical unity. It is one of the regions of France created in the late 20th century to serve as a hinterland and zone of influence for its capital, Toulouse, one of a handful of so-called "balancing metropolises" (métropoles d'équilibre).<ref>In the 1960s, eight large regional cities of France (Toulouse, Lille, Nancy, Strasbourg, Lyon, Nantes, Bordeaux, and Marseille) were made "balancing metropolises", receiving special financial and technical help from the French government in order to counterbalance the excessive weight of Paris inside France.</ref> Another example of this is the region of Rhône-Alpes which was created as the region for Lyon.
