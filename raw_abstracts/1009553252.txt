It was widely accepted that The Representative of Corwood Industries' real name is Sterling Richard Smith a review of the debut album Ready for the House (1978) in OP magazine, the first ever national press given to Jandek, referred to the artist as Sterling Smith.<ref></ref> Smith has kept his personal history an almost absolute secret, revealing only one story about his pre-Corwood years: he wrote seven novels but burned them upon rejection from New York publishers.<ref></ref> Aside from that anecdote, virtually nothing is known about his life. It has also been suggested that he is well traveled, as several album sleeves depict locations that have been formally identified as being outside the United States. 
In one of his three unauthorized interviews (unknowingly given in 1985 to John Trubee for Spin Magazine), The Rep mentioned that he was working at that time as a machinist.<ref name="Trubee"></ref>
