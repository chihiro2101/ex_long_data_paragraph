The Union County Magnet High School (UCMHS) is a magnet public high school located in Scotch Plains on the Union County Vocational Technical Schools Campus, serving the vocational and technical educational needs of students in ninth through twelfth grades throughout Union County, New Jersey, United States. The school's goal is to prepare students for college/vocational training utilizing technology through problem solving, project-based learning, and interdisciplinary education. Students must apply to enter the school and the school accepts one-thirds of applicants. As of the 2018–19 school year, the school had an enrollment of 303 students and 13.5 classroom teachers (on an FTE basis), for a student–teacher ratio of 22.5:1. There were 24 students (7.9% of enrollment) eligible for free lunch and 10 (3.3% of students) eligible for reduced-cost lunch.<ref name=NCES> urltoken School data for Union County Magnet High School, National Center for Education Statistics.
