Halva (also halvah, halwa,<ref name=NYT.Nos></ref><ref></ref> and other spellings) refers to various local confection recipes in West Asia and its vicinity.<ref name="Davidson"></ref><ref></ref><ref name=NYT.BK/> The name is used for referring to a huge variety of confections, with the most geographically common variety based on fried semolina.<ref name="Davidson"/>
Halva is also popular in Central and South Asia, the Balkans, the Caucasus, Eastern Europe, Malta, North Africa and the Horn of Africa. Identical sweets exist in other countries, such as China, though these are not generally referred to as "halva".
Halva can be kept at room temperature during non-summer months with little risk of spoilage.<ref></ref>
