A doxology (Ancient Greek: doxologia, from , doxa, "glory" and -, -logia, "saying")<ref>American Heritage Dictionary, Wordnik, s.v. " urltoken doxology".</ref><ref></ref><ref>.</ref> is a short hymn of praises to God in various forms of Christian worship, often added to the end of canticles, psalms, and hymns. The tradition derives from a similar practice in the Jewish synagogue,<ref> urltoken Doxology - Catholic Encyclopedia article</ref> where some version of the Kaddish serves to terminate each section of the service.
