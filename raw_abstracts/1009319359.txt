An unpublished study in 2019 compared behavior of captive-reared versus wild monarch larvae.<ref></ref> The study showed that reared larvae exhibited more defensive behavior than wild larvae. The reason for this is unknown, but it could relate to the fact that reared larvae are frequently handled and/or disturbed.
