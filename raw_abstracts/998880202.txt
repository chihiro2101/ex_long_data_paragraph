Silane is an inorganic compound with chemical formula, SiH<sub>4</sub>, making it a group 14 hydride. It is a colourless, pyrophoric, toxic gas with a sharp, repulsive smell, somewhat similar to that of acetic acid.<ref> urltoken CFC Startec properties of Silane . C-f-c.com. Retrieved on 2013-03-06.</ref> Silane is of practical interest as a precursor to elemental silicon.
"Silanes" refers to many compounds with four substituents on silicon, including an organosilicon compound. Examples include trichlorosilane (SiHCl<sub>3</sub>), tetramethylsilane (Si(CH<sub>3</sub>)<sub>4</sub>), and tetraethoxysilane (Si(OC<sub>2</sub>H<sub>5</sub>)<sub>4</sub>).
