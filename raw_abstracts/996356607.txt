The riksdaler was the name of a Sweden coin first minted in 1604. Between 1777 and 1873, it was the currency of Sweden. The daler, like the dollar,<ref name="NatGeo">National Geographic. June 2002. p. 1. Ask Us.</ref> was named after the German Thaler. The similarly named Reichsthaler, rijksdaalder, and rigsdaler were used in Germany and Austria-Hungary, the Netherlands, and Denmark-Norway, respectively. Riksdaler is still used as a colloquial term for Swedish krona.<ref></ref>
