The Skagerrak , <small>also</small> ,<ref></ref><ref> urltoken "Skagerrak" (US) and </ref><ref></ref> , , is a strait running between the southeast coast of Norway, the west coast of Sweden, and the Jutland of Denmark, connecting the North Sea and the Kattegat sea area, which leads to the Baltic Sea.
The Skagerrak contains some of the busiest shipping routes in the world, with vessels from every corner of the globe. It also supports an intensive fishing industry.<ref name="NEA"/> The ecosystem is strained and negatively affected by direct human activities. Oslo and Gothenburg are the only large cities in the Skagerrak region.
