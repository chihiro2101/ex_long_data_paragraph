Catharsis (from Greek , , meaning "purification" or "cleansing" or "clarification") is the purification and purgation of emotions—particularly pity and fear—through art<ref></ref> or any extreme change in emotion that results in renewal and restoration.<ref></ref><ref></ref> It is a metaphor originally used by Aristotle in the Poetics, comparing the effects of tragedy on the mind of a spectator to the effect of catharsis on the body.<ref>Aristotle, Poetics, urltoken 1449b</ref><ref></ref>
