Arson is a crime of willfully and maliciously setting fire to or charring property.<ref name="findlaw"></ref> Though the act typically involves buildings, the term arson can also refer to the intentional burning of other things, such as motor vehicles, watercraft, or forests.<ref name="findlaw" /> The crime is typically classified as a felony, with instances involving a greater degree of risk to human life or property carrying a stricter penalty.<ref name="findlaw" /> A common motive for arson is to commit insurance fraud.<ref name="findlaw" /><ref> urltoken arson. A person who commits arson is called an arsonist. Arsonists normally use an accelerant (such as gasoline or kerosene) to ignite, propel and directionalize fires, and the detection and identification of ignitable liquid residues (ILRs) is an important part of fire investigations.<ref name="Almirall"></ref> Pyromania is an impulse control disorder characterized by the pathological setting of fires.<ref name="Burton"></ref> Most acts of arson are not committed by pyromaniacs.<ref name="Burton"/>
