The Dumbarton Oaks Conference or, more formally, the Washington Conversations on International Peace and Security Organization was an international conference at which proposals for the establishment of a "general international organization", which was to become the United Nations, were formulated and negotiated. The conference was led by the Big Four – the United States, United Kingdom, USSR and the Republic of China – with delegates from other nations participating in the consideration and formulation of these principles.<ref name=":1"></ref> It was held at Dumbarton Oaks, in Washington, D.C., from August 21, 1944, to October 7, 1944.
