Gelon also known as Gelo (Greek: Γέλων Gelon, gen.: Γέλωνος; died 478 BC), son of Deinomenes, was a 5th-century BC ruler of Gela and Syracuse and first of the Deinomenid rulers.<ref>According to Bury & Meiggs on pages 156, 158 and 191; de Sélincourt's Herodotus on pages 494–495, etc.; and Britannica, the correct usage of his name in English is Gelon.</ref>
