Alprazolam, sold under the brand name Xanax, among others, is a short-acting tranquilizer of the triazolobenzodiazepine (TBZD) class, which are benzodiazepines (BZDs) fused with a triazole ring.<ref></ref> It is most commonly used in short-term management of anxiety disorders, specifically panic disorder or generalized anxiety disorder (GAD).<ref name="Label2017"></ref> Other uses include the treatment of chemotherapy-induced nausea, together with other treatments.<ref name=AHFS2018/> GAD improvement occurs generally within a week.<ref name=Verster2004></ref><ref></ref> Alprazolam is generally taken by mouth.<ref name=AHFS2018></ref>
Common side effects include sleepiness, depression, headaches, feeling tired, dry mouth, and memory problems.<ref name=AHFS2018/> Some of the sedation and tiredness may improve within a few days.<ref name=Pav10></ref> Due to concerns about misuse, some do not recommend alprazolam as an initial treatment for panic disorder.<ref name= Moy2012></ref> Withdrawal or rebound symptoms may occur if use is suddenly decreased;<ref name=AHFS2018/> gradually decreasing the dose over weeks or months may be required.<ref name="Verster2004" /> Other rare risks include suicide, possibly due to loss of inhibition.<ref name=Dobb2017></ref> Alprazolam, like other benzodiazepines, acts through the GABAA receptor.<ref name=AHFS2018/>
Alprazolam was patented in 1971 and approved for medical use in the United States in 1981.<ref name=AHFS2018/><ref name=Fis2006></ref> Alprazolam is a Schedule IV controlled substance and is a common drug of abuse.<ref name=Ait2018></ref><ref></ref> It is available as a generic medication.<ref name="Label2017" /> In 2018, it was the 37th most commonly prescribed medication in the United States, with more than 20million prescriptions.<ref></ref><ref></ref>
