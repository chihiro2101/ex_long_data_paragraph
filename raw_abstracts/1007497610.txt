Pierre Puvis de Chavannes (14 December 1824&nbsp;– 24 October 1898) was a French painter known for his mural painting, who came to be known as 'the painter for France'.<ref>Brown Price, A., 2010, V1</ref> He became the co-founder and president of the Société Nationale des Beaux-Arts, and his work influenced many other artists, notably Robert Genin. Puvis de Chavannes was a prominent painter in the early Third Republic. Émile Zola described his work as "an art made of reason, passion, and will".<ref>Shaw, J. L., 'Frenchness, Memory and Abstraction: The Case of Pierre Puvis de Chavannes' in Hargrove, J., & McWilliam, N., eds., 2005, Nationalism and French Visual Culture, National Gallery of Art, Washington, Distributed by Yale University Press, New Haven and London, p. 153</ref>
