Count (feminine: countess) is a historical title of nobility in certain European countries, varying in relative status, generally of middling rank in the hierarchy of nobility.<ref name="pine">L. G. Pine Titles: How the King Became His Majesty. New York: Barnes & Noble, 1992. p. 73. .</ref> The etymologically related English term "county" denoted the land owned by a count. Equivalents of the rank of count exist or have existed in the nobility structures of some non-European countries, such as hakushaku during the Empire of Japan.
