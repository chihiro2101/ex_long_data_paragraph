Higuchi was unique among her peers in that her writing was based on Japanese rather than Western models, for which . Born into a minor samurai family, her experience of poverty, particularly after the death of her father in 1889, at which point she took on the responsibility as head of an all female household, informed her writing, which included themes of abandonment and suffering combined with sensitivity. Specializing in short stories, she was one of the first important writers to appear in the Meiji period (1868–1912) and Japan's first prominent woman writer of modern times. She was also an extensive diary. She wrote relatively little as a result of living a brief life — she died at 24 — but her stories have had a large impact on Japanese literature.
