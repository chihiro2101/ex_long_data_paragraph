Nagel taught at the University of California, Berkeley (from 1963 to 1966) and at Princeton University (from 1966 to 1980), where he trained many well-known philosophers including Susan Wolf, Shelly Kagan, and Samuel Scheffler, the latter of whom is now his colleague at NYU.
Nagel is a Fellow of the American Academy of Arts and Sciences and a Corresponding Fellow of the British Academy, and, in 2006, was elected as a member of the American Philosophical Society.<ref name="Nagel"></ref> He has held a fellowship from the Guggenheim Foundation, the National Science Foundation, and the National Endowment for the Humanities.<ref name="Nagel"/> In 2008, he was awarded a Rolf Schock Prize for his work in philosophy,<ref></ref> the Balzan prize,<ref></ref> and the honorary degree of Doctor of Letters from the University of Oxford.<ref></ref>
