Schistosomiasis, also known as snail fever and bilharzia,<ref></ref> is a disease caused by parasitic flatworms called schistosomes.<ref name=WHO2014/> The urinary tract or the intestines may be infected.<ref name=WHO2014/> Symptoms include abdominal pain, diarrhea, bloody stool, or hematuria.<ref name=WHO2014/> Those who have been infected for a long time may experience liver damage, kidney failure, infertility, or bladder cancer.<ref name=WHO2014/> In children, it may cause poor growth and learning disability.<ref name=WHO2014></ref>
The disease is spread by contact with fresh water contaminated with the parasites.<ref name=WHO2014/> These parasites are released from infected freshwater snails.<ref name=WHO2014/> The disease is especially common among children in developing countries, as they are more likely to play in contaminated water.<ref name=WHO2014/> Other high-risk groups include farmers, fishermen, and people using unclean water during daily living.<ref name=WHO2014/> It belongs to the group of helminthiasis.<ref></ref> Diagnosis is by finding eggs of the parasite in a person's urine or stool.<ref name=WHO2014/> It can also be confirmed by finding antibodies against the disease in the blood.<ref name=WHO2014/>
Methods of preventing the disease include improving access to clean water and reducing the number of snails.<ref name=WHO2014/> In areas where the disease is common, the medication praziquantel may be given once a year to the entire group.<ref name=WHO2014/> This is done to decrease the number of people infected, and consequently, the spread of the disease.<ref name=WHO2014/> Praziquantel is also the treatment recommended by the World Health Organization for those who are known to be infected.<ref name=WHO2014/>
Schistosomiasis affected about 252 million people worldwide in 2015.<ref name=GBD2015Pre></ref> An estimated 4,400 to 200,000 people die from it each year.<ref name=The2013></ref><ref name=GBD2015De></ref> The disease is most commonly found in Africa, Asia, and South America.<ref name=WHO2014/> Around 700 million people, in more than 70 countries, live in areas where the disease is common.<ref name=The2013/><ref></ref> In tropical countries, schistosomiasis is second only to malaria among parasitic diseases with the greatest economic impact.<ref name="SCP"></ref> Schistosomiasis is listed as a neglected tropical disease.<ref></ref>
