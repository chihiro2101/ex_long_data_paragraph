Camper Van Beethoven is an American rock band formed in Redlands, California, in 1983, later located in Santa Cruz and San Francisco. Their style mixes elements of pop, ska, punk rock, folk, alternative country, and world music.<ref name="The Great Rock Discography"></ref> The band initially polarized audiences within the hardcore punk scene of California's Inland Empire<ref name=thr></ref> before finding wider acceptance and, eventually, an international audience. Their strong iconoclasm and emphasis on DIY ethic values proved influential to the burgeoning indie rock movement.<ref></ref>
The band's first three independent records were released within an 18-month period. Their debut single was "Take the Skinheads Bowling".<ref></ref> The group signed to Virgin Records in 1987, released two albums and enjoyed chart success with their 1989 cover of Status Quo's "Pictures of Matchstick Men", a number one hit on Billboard Magazines Modern Rock Tracks.<ref></ref> They disbanded the following year due to internal tensions.
