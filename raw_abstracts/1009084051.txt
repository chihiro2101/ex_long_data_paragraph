Robert Louis Johnson (born April 8, 1946) is an American entrepreneur, media magnate, executive, philanthropist, and investor.<ref name=FORB></ref><ref name=bryant></ref><ref name=eng></ref><ref name=perl></ref><ref name=wapost/><ref name=USAT></ref> He is the co-founder of BET, which was acquired by Viacom in 2001.<ref name=bryant /><ref name=eng /> He also founded RLJ Companies, a holding company that invests in various business sectors.<ref name=eng /><ref name=CBSNEWS></ref> Johnson is the former majority owner of the Charlotte Bobcats.<ref name=rhoden></ref> He became the first black American billionaire in 2001.<ref name=":0"></ref><ref name=FORB /><ref name=olson></ref> Johnson's companies have counted among the most prominent African-American businesses in the late twentieth and early twenty-first centuries.
