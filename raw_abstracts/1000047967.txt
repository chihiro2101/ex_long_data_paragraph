Sexual fetishism or erotic fetishism is a sexual fixation on a nonliving object or nongenital body part.<ref name="DSM-5" /> The object of interest is called the fetish; the person who has a fetish for that object is a fetishist.<ref name="vekquin"></ref> A sexual fetish may be regarded as a non-pathological aid to sexual excitement, or as a mental disorder if it causes significant psychosocial distress for the person or has detrimental effects on important areas of their life.<ref name="DSM-5"></ref><ref name="ICD"></ref> Sexual arousal from a particular body part can be further classified as partialism.<ref>Milner, J. S., & Dopke, C. A. (1997). Paraphilia Not Otherwise Specified: Psychopathology and theory. In D. R. Laws and W. O'Donohue (Eds.), Sexual deviance: Theory, assessment, and treatment. New York: Guilford.</ref>
While medical definitions restrict the term sexual fetishism to objects or body parts,<ref name="DSM-5" /> fetish can, in common discourse, also refer to sexual interest in specific activities.<ref name="scorolli"></ref>
