A child prodigy is defined in psychology research literature as a person under the age of ten who produces meaningful output in some domain to the level of an adult expert.<ref></ref><ref name="forbes.com"></ref><ref></ref>
The term wunderkind (from , literally "wonder child") is sometimes used as a synonym for child prodigy, particularly in media accounts. Wunderkind also is used to recognize those who achieve success and acclaim early in their adult careers.<ref name="Merriam-Webster Dictionary"></ref>
