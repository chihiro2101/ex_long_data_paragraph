Whiplash is a non-medical term describing a range of injury to the neck caused by or related to a sudden distortion of the neck<ref></ref> associated with extension,<ref></ref> although the exact injury mechanisms remain unknown. The term "whiplash" is a colloquialism. Before the invention of the car, whiplash injuries were called "railway spine" as they were noted mostly in connection with train collisions. The first case of severe neck pain arising from a train collision was documented around 1919.<ref name=Des10></ref> The number of whiplash injuries has since risen sharply due to rear-end motor vehicle collisions. Given the wide variety of symptoms associated with whiplash injuries, the Quebec Task Force on Whiplash-Associated Disorders coined the phrase 'Whiplash-Associated Disorders'.<ref name=Des10/>
