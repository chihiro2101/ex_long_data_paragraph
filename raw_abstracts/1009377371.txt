A <ref name="britannica"></ref><ref></ref> was a samurai without a lord or master during the feudal period (1185–1868) of Japan. A samurai became masterless upon the death of his master or after the loss of his master's favor or privilege.<ref>Barry Till, urltoken "The 47 Ronin: A Story of Samurai Loyalty and Courage", 2005, pg. 11</ref>
In modern Japanese usage, sometimes the term is used to describe a salaryman who is unemployed or Rōnin (student).<ref>Akihiko Yonekawa. Beyond Polite Japanese. page 25. Kodansha 2001. </ref><ref>浪人 at Japanese-English dictionaries: urltoken プログレッシブ和英中辞典 or urltoken ニューセンチュリー和英辞典 </ref>
